About AgilePredict

AgilePredict forecasts wholesale day-ahead electricity prices and converts those forecasts into Octopus Agile import prices for each supported region. The raw day-ahead price is also available as Region Z and is shown in £/MWh.

The model is trained on historical prices, demand, generation forecasts, and weather inputs. Forecasts are generated by an equal-weight ensemble of CatBoost, LightGBM, and Extra Trees regressors. A confidence band (p10–p90) accompanies each forecast, combining empirical residuals from historical out-of-sample predictions with spread from a weather ensemble.

temp_2m 2 m temperature forecast Open-Meteo

Weather inputs are sourced from the Open-Meteo forecast API at 15-minute resolution and resampled to 30 minutes.

How Forecasts Are Built

  1. Recent forecasts and actual prices are loaded from the local database.
  2. Training rows are selected from the PM auction window, aligned around the 16:15 update.
  3. CatBoost, LightGBM, and Extra Trees models are each fitted on the training data; their predictions are averaged to produce the day-ahead forecast.
  4. Predictions are blended with known actual prices where appropriate.
  5. Regional Agile prices are derived from the day-ahead forecast using the configured region scale and shift factors.
  6. Confidence bands are added using empirical residuals and weather ensemble spread (see below).

Comparison Forecasts

The History page compares AgilePredict against stored external forecasts from AgileForecast and X2R. X2R does not publish a direct National Average region, so the Region X comparison is inferred from Region G using the same configured scale and shift logic used elsewhere in AgilePredict.

External forecasts are for comparison only. They do not feed into the published AgilePredict model.

Forecast Uncertainty Bands

The shaded band around each forecast represents a p10–p90 interval: in principle, the actual price should fall inside the band for roughly 80% of half-hour slots.

The band has two components. First, empirical residuals from held-out historical forecasts are binned by forecast horizon (6 h, 12 h, 24 h, 36 h, 48 h) to set a baseline floor that grows with look-ahead distance. Second, the ICON seamless weather ensemble from Open-Meteo (10 members) is run through the price model; the spread of those member predictions widens the band on days where weather is uncertain. The two components are combined by taking the wider of the two at each settlement period.

Bands are smoothed with a three-period rolling average and always enclose the point forecast.

Updates and Accuracy

Forecasts are updated automatically during the day, with the main update aligned to the day-ahead auction cycle. Accuracy is usually better for broad price shape and daily level than for exact half-hour prices, especially further into the forecast horizon.

AgilePredict is an open-source forecasting project. Please use the forecasts with care and check official pricing when the decision matters.
